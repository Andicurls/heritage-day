(Heritage Day) is a holiday which is celebrated on the 24th September,it is a special occasion dedicated to cherishing and honouring all the diverse cultures in South Africa that make our country so beautiful and vibrant.
This holiday serves as a unifying force that brings all South Africans together as we appreaciate and learn about each other's different cultures.
We invite you to join us as we celebrate heritage day and embark on a journey of new discovery as we educate ourselves on the many vibrant cultures.

Fun Facts About Different Cultures

  • The complex beadwork found in Zulu traditional attire carries multiple meanings.
    For instance, white beads symbolize purity and love and green represents contentment.
    Additionally, these beads convey important information about gender and one's family background.
    •  
  • Face painting, known as umchokozo, holds significant importance in Xhosa culture. Women decorate their faces with white or yellow ochre, creating intricate patterns such as dots.
    These designs may extend over their eyebrows, the bridge of their noses, and cheeks.
    These designs are said to have spiritual and cutural significance ,instill fear in enemies in adverseries.
    •  
  • Afrikaans is one of the 11 official languages in South Africa which has most of it's words originating from Dutch.
    The Afrikaners respect the history of their culture and in doing so enjoy sports such as Rugby, Golf as well as traditional barbequed meats.
